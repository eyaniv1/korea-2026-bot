What We'd Be Missing — Worth Considering

TIER 1: Don't Skip These (Easy to Add)

1. DMZ Tour — Half day from Seoul
The most unique-to-Korea experience on Earth. Third Infiltration Tunnel, Dora Observatory overlooking North Korea, Imjingak Park. Guided tour required (~$40/person). The geopolitical dimension will resonate deeply for an Israeli family. Book 1–2 weeks ahead.
2. Jjimjilbang / Korean Spa — Any evening
Quintessential Korean cultural experience. Communal baths, themed saunas (clay, ice, salt rooms), heated floors for napping, slow-cooked eggs. Under $15 entry. Dragon Hill Spa in Yongsan is the classic. Both generations will love it.
3. Hanbok at Gyeongbokgung — During Day 2 palace visit
Rent traditional Korean dress ($10–50/person, 2–4 hrs). Wearing hanbok gives FREE palace entry. Great photos, especially for the young couple. Dozens of rental shops surround the palace. Zero schedule change needed.
4. Samgwangsa Temple Lanterns (Busan) — During Busan days
40,000 lotus lanterns lit up for Buddha's Birthday season. Free, spectacular, and happening exactly when you're there. Add to a Busan evening.

TIER 2: Highly Recommended (Requires Some Reshuffling)

5. Jeonju Hanok Village — Full day trip
Korea's food capital and birthplace of bibimbap. 800+ traditional hanok houses with teahouses, craft shops, street food. The Jeonju-style bibimbap at Hankookjib (since 1952) is the best in the country. 1.5 hrs by KTX.
6. Korean Cooking Class — Half day
Market tour + hands-on cooking of bibimbap, kimchi, japchae. ~$50/person, 3–4 hours. Highly rated family bonding activity.
7. Suwon Hwaseong Fortress — Half day from Seoul
UNESCO World Heritage Site, 30–50 min from Seoul. 5.7 km wall walk past gates, watchtowers, and Haenggung Palace. Perfect for walking and culture lovers.
8. Temple Stay — Overnight
Sleep in a Buddhist monastery, 3 AM chanting, temple food, meditation, tea ceremony. ~$50–75/night. Transformative but intense. Best for the adventurous.

TIER 3: Excellent If Time Allows

9. Damyang Bamboo Forest + Metasequoia Road
Korea's most famous bamboo forest (310,000 sqm) plus a stunning tree-lined boulevard. Fresh green leaves in late April. Can combine with Jeonju.
10. Seoraksan National Park
Korea's most dramatic mountain scenery — granite peaks, waterfalls, azaleas. 3+ hours from Seoul. Spectacular for hikers, but you already have plenty of trails on this trip.
11. Andong Hahoe Village — UNESCO Heritage
Living Joseon-era village, 600+ years old. Famous Hahoe Mask Dance Drama. More authentic than most hanok villages. Works as a stop between Gyeongju and Seoul.
12. Suncheon Bay Garden & Wetlands
Korea's first national garden + coastal wetlands. Tulips, azaleas, canola in full bloom late April. Beautiful for nature lovers, but far south (Jeolla Province).

Zero-Effort Food Additions

13. Chimaek by the Han River
Korean fried chicken + beer, eaten on mats by the river at night. Peak Korean lifestyle. Do this on your Han River evening.
14. Samgyetang at Tosokchon
Ginseng chicken soup near Gyeongbokgung. Whole chicken stuffed with ginseng, jujubes, garlic, rice. Seafood-free. Perfect lunch on your palace day.
